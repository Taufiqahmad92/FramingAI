The below is attributable to U.S. Mission to the United Nations Spokesperson Lauren French:
Ambassador Linda Thomas-Greenfield, the U.S. Representative to the United Nations, met today with the Secretary-General of the Association of Southeast Asian Nations (ASEAN) Dr. Kao Kim Hourn in Jakarta, Indonesia. Ambassador Thomas-Greenfield reaffirmed the United States' commitment to ASEAN centrality and support for its role in promoting peace, prosperity, and sustainable development in the Indo-Pacific and beyond.
The Ambassador and Secretary-General highlighted areas of cooperation and progress under the U.S.-ASEAN Comprehensive Strategic Partnership, including on clean energy transition, disaster management, maritime security, and the development of artificial intelligence. Ambassador Thomas-Greenfield expressed concern over the deepening crisis in Burma and the People's Republic of China's dangerous and unlawful actions in the South China Sea. Ambassador Thomas-Greenfield and the Secretary-General also discussed how to leverage the U.S.-ASEAN partnership to deliver results for our combined one billion people.
